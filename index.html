Our Fredericton Squadron newsletter returns after 13 year “Turn in”

The Binnacle—the cherished newsletter capturing the rippling yarns, current charts, and riverine rambles of boating aficionados along New Brunswick's storied Saint John River—has joyfully returned this November, as The Helm. For river rats who've longed for its weathered pages.

Chuckles on the Poop Deck

An expensive snore cure

A woman was nearing the end of her tether – every night her husband snored so loudly that it kept her awake. She decided to call the family doctor to see if there was anything that could be done to relieve her nightly suffering.

“Well, there is one operation I can perform that will cure your husband of his snoring,” said the doctor, “but I must warn you that it is rather expensive. It will cost you a deposit of $50,000, and payments of $2,000 for 60 months, as well as money for extras.”

“Good grief!” exclaimed the woman. “That sounds like I’m buying a yacht!”

“Hmm,” the doctor murmured, “too obvious, huh?”

CanBoat / NautiSavoir

A Community Of Experienced Boaters Inspiring Others

CanBoat / NautiSavoir, operated by Canadian Power and Sail Squadrons / Escadrilles canadiennes de plaisance (CPS-ECP), is a national, non-profit boating organization offering courses and seminars for recreational boaters.

Our CanBoat.ca website offers the above certifications and links to its numerous other courses. These build from the basic courses required by law to more advanced material for boaters who would like to cross blue ocean waters.

The PCOC card program is the only Canadian-issued card that qualifies for acceptance by the U.S. Coast Guard. These courses are available online by self-study or taught via instructor led virtual classes or in classrooms.

Since 1938, generations of volunteers have been sharing their on-the-water knowledge with new and seasoned boaters, helping them prepare for both every-day and emergency situations. CanBoat / NautiSavoir courses are developed by boaters for boaters. Membership in CanBoat / NautiSavoir offers access to insurance discounts for boats, home and auto, discounts on courses, on marine wear and products, on marine on the water assistance and more. As well, annual membership dues from individuals allow CanBoat / NautiSavoir to better serve the boating community and develop its programs.

Safety Buoy – Tip of the Month

“Know Before You Go”

Before setting sail, always check the marine weather forecast. Sudden changes in wind and wave conditions can turn a pleasant cruise into a risky situation. Bookmark the Canadian Marine Weather Service and keep a VHF radio tuned to channel 16.

Man Overboard Procedure

1. Shout “MAN OVERBOARD!” + port/starboard
2. Throw life ring & deploy flotation device
3. Assign dedicated spotter – never lose visual
4. Activate MOB on GPS/plotter
5. Execute Williamson turn or immediate return maneuver
6. Deploy rescue boat if conditions allow

Fire Response Protocol

1. Sound continuous fire alarm
2. Announce location over PA
3. Attempt to extinguish with nearest extinguisher (if safe)
4. Close doors & vents to contain fire
5. Activate fixed CO₂ or water mist system (if applicable)
6. Prepare for possible evacuation

Medical Emergency

1. Call “MEDICAL EMERGENCY” + location
2. Administer first aid / CPR if trained
3. Retrieve AED and medical kit
4. Contact shore medical advisory service via radio/satellite
5. Prepare for medevac if required

Abandon Ship Procedure

1. Commander orders “ABANDON SHIP”
2. Sound 7 short + 1 long blast
3. Don life jackets
4. Transmit Mayday on VHF Ch. 16 & SSB
5. Launch liferafts & survival suits
6. Take EPIRB, SART, grab bag, and water
7. Stay together and await rescue

Bearings & Tales – Member Adventures

The YouTube channel Beyond the Buoys (@BeyondthebuoyswithAnM), hosted by Andrea and Mike, both are Fredericton Squadron members, is a vlog series launched in June 2025, centered on real-life boating adventures along Canada's scenic waterways. With a focus on the "highs, lows, and everything in between of life on the water," it shares personal stories of exploration, from coastal anchorages to navigating challenging routes. The channel's debut video introduces their 1979 Mainship MK1 trawler (a 34-foot vessel with diesel engine, full living quarters, and swim platform) and outlines an 18-day delivery trip from Mariposa Marina in St. André d’Argenteuil, Québec, to their home in New Brunswick—covering the St. Lawrence River, Gaspé Peninsula, Northumberland Strait, Canso Strait, southern Nova Scotia, and the Bay of Fundy via reversing falls in Saint John.

Subsequent episodes provide daily-ish updates on the journey, including lock transits, weather hurdles, speed logs, and stunning scenery, in a casual, enthusiastic style that's approachable for boating newcomers or armchair travelers.
