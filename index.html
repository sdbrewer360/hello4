The Helm – November 2025

Our Fredericton Squadron newsletter returns after 13 year “Turn in”

The Binnacle—the cherished newsletter capturing the rippling yarns, current charts, and riverine rambles of boating aficionados along New Brunswick's storied Saint John River—has joyfully returned this November, as The Helm. Once a go-to guide for cruisers meandering past the heritage charm of Fredericton, and anglers casting lines in the tidal reaches near Reversing Falls.

This revival anchors just after the autumn leaves blaze along the banks, brimming with fresh tales of final salmon runs, winterized wooden dories, and the crisp hush of frost-kissed sunrises over the Kennebecasis Valley. For river rats who've longed for its weathered pages.

“The Helm” is provided in a mobile friendly format.
Enjoy, mates!

Steven Brewer, Communications Officer, Fredericton Squadron

Safety Buoy – Tip of the Month

“Know Before You Go”

Always check the Canadian Marine Weather Service and keep VHF tuned to Channel 16.

Man Overboard Procedure

1. Shout “MAN OVERBOARD!” + port/starboard
2. Throw life ring & deploy flotation
3. Assign dedicated spotter
4. Activate MOB on GPS
5. Execute Williamson turn
6. Deploy rescue boat if safe

Fire Response Protocol

1. Sound continuous fire alarm
2. Announce location over PA
3. Attempt to extinguish (if safe)
4. Close doors & vents
5. Activate fixed system
6. Prepare for evacuation

Medical Emergency

1. Call “MEDICAL EMERGENCY” + location
2. Administer first aid / CPR
3. Retrieve AED and medical kit
4. Contact shore medical service
5. Prepare for medevac

Abandon Ship Procedure

1. Commander orders “ABANDON SHIP”
2. Sound 7 short + 1 long blast
3. Don life jackets
4. Transmit Mayday
5. Launch liferafts
6. Take EPIRB, SART, water
7. Stay together and await rescue
